Lead

Steps
1
The First Call
Whether inbound or a return call, open warmly and make the buyer feel immediately welcome. Note: agency disclosure is handled at first substantial contact — for buyers, this typically happens at the beginning of the consultation rather than on the first call, since the call is often brief and exploratory.
2
Discovery
Get a sense of where they are in the process — are they just starting out, actively searching, or ready to move quickly? Understand their general search area, timeline, and motivation.
Note: Listen more than you talk. What you learn here shapes the consultation, the search criteria, and how you position DBRE's value. This is where genuine connection begins.
3
Collect Email and Introduce the Next Step
  • If the buyer called in: ask for their email directly
  • If returning their call and they are driving: ask them to text their email when they get a chance
  • If you are driving when returning a call: pull over or wait before collecting information
4
Send the Buyer Information Sheet
Send the DBRE Client Portal link alongside the Buyer Information Sheet email, within the same business day — ideally within the hour. Your email should include:
  • A warm thank-you for reaching out
  • A link to the Online Buyer Information Sheet
  • The HOME Buyer Guide as an attachment
  • A clear statement that upon receipt you will be in touch to schedule the consultation
Note: Keep this email focused — one clear action for the buyer. The Buyer Consultation Guide is sent with the day-before reminder, when it will actually be read.
Client Portal: Include the DBRE Client Portal link (portal.danablanksrealestate.com) in the first email: "Here is our client resource portal — the Buyer Readiness assessment will help you think through where you are in the process before we meet." During active search, the Mortgage Calculator and Communities guide are particularly useful. The portal remains their resource hub throughout the relationship.

Buyer Consultation

Steps
1
Schedule the Consultation
Upon receipt of the Buyer Information Sheet, reach out promptly. Review the completed form before reaching out — reference it to show you read it. Format is at your discretion — virtual or in-person.
Key Action: Send the Buyer Consultation Confirmation email (Message Center) immediately upon booking.
2
Send Calendar Invite
  • Include date, time, format (virtual link or address), and a brief agenda
  • Confirm what to expect: a walkthrough of the buying process, getting clear on their goals, and next steps
3
Day-Before Reminder — Attach Buyer Consultation Guide
  • Send a warm, brief reminder confirming the time and expressing that you are looking forward to it
  • Attach the Buyer Consultation Guide — ask them to review it before the session
4
Conduct the Buyer Consultation
Open with the Working with Real Estate Agents brochure — NC requires agency disclosure at first substantial contact. Then move into your Buyer Consultation Presentation.
Compliance: Review the Working with Real Estate Agents brochure at the beginning of the consultation before discussing property specifics, search criteria, or financial details.
  • Walk through the Buyer Consultation Presentation — explain the buying process, DBRE's role, and what to expect at each stage
  • Deep-dive into their search criteria: location, price, style, must-haves, and deal-breakers
  • Discuss financing — confirm pre-approval status or refer to a trusted lender if needed
  • Explain your communication style and how you will keep them informed throughout the search
  • Listen carefully — understand not just what they want in a home, but why
Note: The consultation is where genuine connection is built. The buyer should leave feeling that you understand them — not just their search criteria, but their life, their timeline, and what this move means to them.

Phase 4

Home Search Setup

Once the Buyer Agency Agreement is signed, you become the buyer's primary source for properties. Set up their private MLS subscription immediately — and position it that way. A buyer who looks to you first stops needing Zillow.

Steps
1
Set Up Private MLS Subscription in Hive MLS (Flexmls)
  • Navigate to Quick Search on the main dashboard
  • Enter the buyer's specific criteria based on what you learned in the consultation: location, price, bedrooms, structural preferences
  • Click Save → Save Search in the upper-right corner
  • Name the search clearly (e.g., [Buyer Name]'s Home Search)
  • Link to the buyer's contact profile — existing or create new
  • Click Save and Add Subscription
  • Set notification frequency: ASAP is recommended for active buyers in competitive markets
Required — Buyer Opt-In: Hive MLS enforces a strict opt-in privacy policy for all new contacts. The system will automatically send a verification link to the buyer's email. The buyer must click this link to authorize property update emails. Follow up to confirm they received and clicked the opt-in link before assuming the subscription is active.
2
Frame the Search as a Private Service
Do not just send the opt-in link without context. Call or email the buyer to walk them through what to expect. The way you position the subscription determines whether they think of it — and of you — as their primary source.
Luxury Service Standard — "I've set up a private search portal for you. The moment a property that matches what we discussed comes to market, you'll be among the first to know — before most buyers even see it. No scrolling through sites that show outdated information. I've got that covered for you. When something catches your eye, come to me first and I'll dig into the details."
3
Confirm Opt-In and Set Communication Expectations
  • Follow up to confirm the buyer received and clicked the verification link
  • Let them know what to do when something interests them: contact you directly rather than scheduling a showing through a third-party site
  • Set the expectation that you will be in regular contact throughout the active search
4
Weekly Active Search Check-In
While the buyer is in active search mode, stay proactively in front of them. Do not wait for them to reach out.
  • Send a brief weekly check-in — share your thoughts on what has come to market, what's moved under contract, and anything relevant to their search
  • This keeps you top of mind and positions you as the expert, not just a portal they log into
  • If nothing new has come to market that week: say so, and tell them what you're watching
Note: A buyer who hears from you regularly stops needing to check Zillow. Proactive communication is the single most effective way to remain their primary source throughout the search.

Steps
1
Buyer Identifies a Property
When a buyer flags a property from their MLS subscription or otherwise, respond promptly. This is the moment your availability and responsiveness communicate your standard of service.
2
Schedule Showing via Broker Bay
  • Contact the listing agent through Broker Bay to request a showing appointment
  • Confirm date, time, and any access instructions
  • Schedule multiple properties in a single outing when practical — respect the buyer's time
3
Send Showing Invite to Buyer
  • Send a calendar invite confirming the date, time, and address of each property
  • Include any access notes or instructions for the buyer
  • Remind them to bring questions — you'll be there to walk through each property with them
4
Conduct the Showing — Branded Showing Kit
Arrive before the buyer when possible. Present the branded showing kit at the first showing — this is theirs to keep throughout the search.
Luxury Service Touchpoint — The DBRE branded showing kit includes a notepad, pen, and bottled water for each buyer. It is presented at the first showing as part of how DBRE does business. A buyer who has their own notepad takes better notes, gives better feedback, and remembers the experience differently than one who doesn't. Small details communicate that you thought about them before they arrived.
  • Walk through each property thoughtfully — point out features that align with their stated priorities
  • Share your honest professional observations — positive and constructive
  • Let the buyer explore and react; don't oversell
5
Collect Feedback Verbally During the Showing
Gather the buyer's impressions while you are still in the property — reactions are most honest and detailed in the moment.
  • Ask directly: "What did you think? What stood out — positively and negatively?"
  • Note their responses — these calibrate the search going forward
  • If a property is clearly not a fit: acknowledge it, understand why, and use it to sharpen the criteria
6
Same-Day Advisor Follow-Up
After showings, send a brief same-day text or email sharing your thoughts on what you toured — not a form, not a survey. A personal note.
Luxury Service Standard — "Here are my thoughts on the three we saw today..." — a short, personal message that shows you were paying attention and are thinking on their behalf. Most advisors say nothing until the buyer calls. You don't wait.

Steps
1
Pre-Offer Research
Before preparing the offer, complete your due diligence on the property. This protects the buyer and informs your offer strategy.
  • Contact the listing agent to request Cooperating Compensation (Form 220)
  • Research comparable properties to verify the asking price is in line with the market
  • Check tax records to verify ownership
  • Download the Residential Property Disclosure (RPD) and Mineral Oil and Gas Disclosure (MOG) from the MLS
2
Call the Buyer — Walk Through the Offer Together
Before drafting anything, get on a call or meet with the buyer to align on all terms. This is not just a courtesy — it prevents revisions and ensures the buyer is fully informed before signing.
  • Discuss and agree on: offer price, due diligence fee and period, earnest money, closing date, and any additional terms or contingencies
  • Share your professional recommendation on offer price based on your comp research
  • Explain due diligence and earnest money clearly — do not assume the buyer understands these
Luxury Service Standard — "I've put together the offer based on everything we discussed — I want to walk you through it together before we send anything. Do you have 15 minutes today?" This is not optional. Buyers who are walked through their offer make better decisions and feel genuinely taken care of.
3
Prepare the Offer Package
  • Prepare the purchase offer reflecting the agreed-upon terms
  • Send to the buyer for signatures along with the RPD, MOG, and Form 220
  • Walk the buyer through each document before requesting signatures — do not just send for blind signing
4
Submit to Listing Agent
  • Send the signed offer, RPD, MOG, and executed Cooperating Compensation Form 220 to the listing agent
  • Confirm receipt with the listing agent
  • Upload the MLS sheet and all submitted documents to your boardroom
  • Let the buyer know the offer has been submitted and set timeline expectations for a response
Luxury Service Standard — After submitting, send the buyer a brief message: "Your offer has been submitted. Here's what happens next and when we should expect to hear back." Buyers are anxious in this window. You eliminate that anxiety by being proactive.
5
Negotiate
  • Negotiate offer price and terms with the listing agent on the buyer's behalf
  • Keep the buyer informed at every step — they should never feel they are waiting in the dark
  • Once a deal is reached: revise the contract to accurately reflect all negotiated terms
Multiple Offer Situation? Refer to the DBRE Multiple Offer Protocol for the full buyer-side process — including how to advise on strategy, when to use an escalation clause, and how to keep your buyer calm and competitive throughout.
6
Execute the Contract and Hand Off
  • Obtain all buyer signatures on the final executed contract
  • Ensure the listing agent has the fully executed contract
  • Upload all executed documents to your boardroom
  • Complete the Transaction Initiation Form — this is the official TC handoff
  • Forward all documents to the Transaction Coordinator
Luxury Service Standard — Mark the moment. A brief congratulatory call or handwritten note acknowledging that the buyer is under contract goes a long way. This milestone deserves recognition — not just a document for signature.
Continuing from here — Once the Transaction Initiation Form is submitted, the transaction moves to the DBRE Buyer-Side Transaction Workflow SOP — inspection through post-closing.
