Lead

Steps
1
The First Call
Whether inbound or a return call, open warmly and make the seller feel immediately at ease.
2
Agency Disclosure
Before discussing any property specifics, pricing, or motivation, NC requires agency disclosure at first substantial contact.
Compliance: Do not discuss property details, seller motivation, or pricing before completing agency disclosure.
3
Discovery — Understanding the Why
Move beyond property details. Understand what is motivating the seller, their timeline, and what matters most to them about this move. Listen more than you talk.
4
Collect Email and Introduce the Next Step
  • If the seller called in: ask for their email directly
  • If returning their call and they are driving: ask them to text you their email when they get a chance
  • If you are driving when returning a call: pull over or wait before collecting information
5
Send the Seller Information Form
Send within the same business day — ideally within the hour. Your email should include:
  • A warm thank-you for reaching out
  • A link to the Online Seller Information Form
  • The SOLD Seller Guide as an attachment
  • A clear statement that upon receipt you will be in touch to schedule the Strategy Session
Note: Keep this email focused — one clear action for the seller. The Seller Consultation Guide is sent separately with the Strategy Session confirmation, when the relationship has momentum.

Phase 2

Strategy Session Preparation

Arrive having done the work. A prepared advisor communicates competence and respect for the seller's time. The Strategy Session is both a presentation and a conversation — deliver expert guidance, then listen.

Steps
1
Schedule the Strategy Session
Upon receipt of the Seller Information Form, reach out promptly. Review the form before reaching out — reference it in your communication to show you read it.
  • Confirm whether virtual or in-person at the property
  • Confirm objectives: goals, timeline, property condition
Key Action: Send the Strategy Session Confirmation email (Message Center › 01 The Listing Journey › 01 The Discovery Phase) immediately upon booking. Attach the Seller Consultation Guide to this email — it gives the seller context on the process, pricing, and what to expect before you meet.
2
Prepare for the Strategy Session
  • Complete the Seller Estimated Net Sheet — sellers decide based on what they walk away with, not just list price
  • Run a full CMA — sold comparables, active competition, and market trends
  • Prepare your listing presentation
  • Review the completed Seller Information Form once more for details to address
3
Send Invite and Day-Before Reminder
  • Calendar invite: date, time, format (virtual link or address), brief agenda
  • Day-before reminder: short and warm — confirm the time, express you are looking forward to it
4
Conduct the Strategy Session
  • Walk through the CMA — explain your methodology, not just the number
  • Present the Seller Estimated Net Sheet and discuss realistic expectations
  • Deliver your listing presentation — highlight DBRE process, marketing approach, and communication standards
  • Discuss staging, photography, and timeline expectations
  • Listen carefully. Understand what success looks like beyond price.
Note: The Strategy Session is where genuine connection is built or missed. Ask one more question than you think you need to. Then listen.

Steps
1
Communicate Next Steps Post-Strategy Session
  • If ready to sign at the meeting: have the listing agreement available and walk through it together
  • If sending electronically: commit to sending the next day for e-signature — and honor it
2
Execute the Listing Agreement
  • Use the Exclusive Right to Sell Listing Agreement in Zipforms — confirm current version
  • Review all key terms: list price, commission, listing period, showing access, seller concessions
  • Execute via electronic signature
Deadline: Listings must be entered into MLS within two days of the listing agreement being signed.
3
Leave Disclosure Form with Seller
  • Residential: Residential Property Disclosure form
  • Land: Vacant Land Disclosure form
  • Instruct seller to complete and return as soon as possible
4
Upload and Log
  • Upload executed listing agreement to your boardroom along with all accompanying documentation
  • Add seller's contact information to your Contact Import Spreadsheet
5
Request Sign, Lockbox, and Access
  • Email operations@danablanksrealestate.com to request sign and lockbox
  • Install yard sign and lockbox at the property
  • Residential only: obtain house key from seller and place in lockbox
  • Confirm access is set up correctly before leaving the property

Steps
1
Complete MLS Listing Input Form and Schedule Photography
  • Use the residential or land form as applicable — accuracy in every field matters
  • Schedule professional photography immediately — non-negotiable for a DBRE listing
  • Upload all photos to the property file in your boardroom
  • MLS requires at least one photo to make a listing live
2
Home Office Makes the Listing Live — Target Thursdays
Home Office handles MLS activation. Your role is to have everything prepared and submitted in advance.
  • Review every MLS field and confirm photo order — hero shot first
  • Confirm showing instructions with the seller before the listing goes live
3
Sentrilock, Broker Bay, and Seller Communications
  • Open your Sentrilock app and confirm the lockbox is assigned to the property
  • Call Broker Bay and provide the confirmed showing instructions
  • Send the Next Steps email template (Message Center) — tailored
  • Send the Your Listing Is Live email template (Message Center) — tailored
4
Activate the Marketing Plan
  • Prepare and schedule at least two social media pieces
  • Schedule an open house for the first nice-weather weekend
  • Send email marketing to other brokers — copy the seller
  • Prepare neighborhood mailing
  • Prepare property flyers for inside the home
Note: Copying the seller on broker email marketing shows them DBRE is actively working their listing and builds confidence in your process.
5
Ongoing Listing Management
  • Coordinate showing approvals and denials promptly with the seller
  • Follow up with each showing agent within 24–48 hours for feedback
  • Relay feedback with context — do not just forward raw comments
  • Monitor competing listings, new contracts, and online activity weekly
  • Send the Weekly Connection email every Friday — use the Message Center template, tailored
  • Call the seller every other week in addition to the Friday email
  • If activity is not converting to offers: initiate a pricing conversation proactively — do not wait for the seller to raise it
Note: The Weekly Connection email was promised in your listing presentation. Delivering it every Friday without the seller asking is the luxury service standard in action.

Offer & Under Contract

Steps
1
Offer Received — Contact Seller Same Day
  • Call the seller the same day — do not let an offer sit unacknowledged
  • Do not read the full offer over the phone — arrange a same-day review time
  • Confirm their availability and set the time before ending the call
2
Review Offer and Advise
  • Review in full: price, financing, contingencies, earnest money, closing date, unusual terms
  • Prepare your recommendation and be ready to explain the reasoning
  • Walk the seller through every material term — do not assume they understand industry language
  • Offer honest professional opinion on strengths, risks, and areas to negotiate
3
Negotiate
You lead the negotiation independently. Approach with preparation, composure, and the seller's goals front of mind.
  • Know the seller's bottom line and priorities before countering
  • Counter strategically — protect the seller's position without unnecessary friction
  • Keep the seller informed at every step — they should never feel they are waiting in the dark
Multiple Offer Situation? Refer to the DBRE Multiple Offer Protocol for the full seller-side process — including how to notify all buyers, set the highest and best deadline, present offers to the seller, and communicate the outcome to all parties.
4
Execute the Contract
Once terms are agreed upon, the buyer's agent updates the contract to negotiated terms. Review carefully before proceeding to signatures.
  • Confirm all negotiated terms are accurately reflected before sending for signatures
  • Obtain seller signatures on all documents
  • Upload the executed contract to your boardroom
  • Send a copy of the executed contract to the buyer's agent
5
Due Diligence (If Applicable)
  • Make arrangements with the buyer's agent to collect due diligence
  • Deliver due diligence to the seller promptly upon receipt
  • Confirm earnest money delivery deadline with the cooperating agent
6
TC Handoff and Under Contract Marketing
  • Complete the Transaction Initiation Form — this is the official TC handoff
  • Inform your BIC (Dana) so MLS can be updated to Under Contract
  • Prepare and schedule your Under Contract social media templates immediately
Continuing from here — Once the Transaction Initiation Form is submitted and MLS is updated, the transaction moves to the DBRE Seller-Side Transaction Workflow SOP — Due Diligence through Post-Closing.
